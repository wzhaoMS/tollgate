# Evidence Grading A / B / C / D

The single most important upgrade vs Serenity's process. She mixes evidence tiers
freely; we do not.

## Tiered rubric

| Grade | Source bar | Sizing permission |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Customer's own SEC filing (10-K / 10-Q / 8-K / S-1) **explicitly** uses words like "sole source", "primary supplier", "strategic supplier", or names X by name as material to operations | Core position |
| **B** | Supplier's annual report names the customer, AND customer's website / partner page / press release independently confirms | Small position |
| **C** | Twitter, conference slides, job postings on LinkedIn, recent patents, supplier capacity hints — anything circumstantial | Observation only |
| **D** | Community speculation, anonymous screenshots, AI hallucinations, "I heard from someone…" | Do not buy |

## Worked examples (illustrative)

| Claim | Grade | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| "SIVE is Ayar's sole CW-laser supplier" | A if Ayar prospectus says it, else B/C | Need to read Ayar's own filing (when public) |
| "XFAB is the only high-volume SiC foundry in the US" | A | NIST + Dept. of Commerce officially named them |
| "NOK is a likely customer of SIVE" (from SIVE's annual report) | C (one-sided) | Supplier-only mention without customer confirmation |
| "AAOI ships to MSFT Maia program" | B if AAOI 10-K says, A if MSFT 10-K confirms | — |
| "NVDA invested $1B in NOK" | A | SEC 8-K from NOK confirms |
| "Anonymous trader said XYZ" | D | — |

## Anti-patterns

- Stacking 3 × C-grade items and calling it "convergent evidence" — still C.
- Treating "in evaluation" as production-quality evidence — that is C at best.
- Trusting community screenshots of supposed FCC / FDA / NIST docs without
  pulling the original ourselves.

## When to upgrade evidence

A C-grade thesis can become a B if:
- A customer issues a press release naming the supplier publicly
- Both companies confirm partnership in their next earnings call
- An independent industry analyst (Dell'Oro, Cignal AI, Omdia) cites the relationship

A B-grade thesis becomes A only when **the customer's own filing** says so.
The supplier's filing is never enough to reach A.

## When to downgrade evidence

Drop a grade if:
- The customer recently announced a competing supplier
- The supplier renegotiated to lower exclusivity
- The customer added the supplier to a list of multiple sources
- Time has passed without revenue ramp (stale evidence = lower grade)
